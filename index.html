About Me

I am an Assistant Professor of Economics at the International School of Economics and Management, Capital University of Economics and Business.

My primary research interests include Quantitative / Mathematical Economics and Macroeconomics. My recent research projects are concerned with wealth inequality and related economic policies, with a particular focus on the quantitative impact of capital income risk on wealth inequality.

I am a huge fan of mathematics and programming (Python/Julia). My secondary research interests include Dynamic Programming Theory and Stochastic Stability.
